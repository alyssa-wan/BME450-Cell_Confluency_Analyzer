# Project description
The goal of this project is to create an analyzer network that can accurately determine the confluence of cells in an experiment. Confluence is the percentage of a culture vessel surface that is covered by adherent cells. It's important to perform experiments, as overconfluency may lead to nutrient depletion and contact inhibition, which can cause incorrect results. Currently, the process relies on qualitative observations, with teams of scientists looking at and estimating the confluence, which can lead to inconsistent reproducibility and skewed experimental results.

For our project, we are planning on using a mix of our own and our classmates' cell culture images from our previous biomaterial lab (BME220) to train and test our model. Our goal is to reliably output the percent of confluence of a given culture and determine whether the cells in the culture are viable for further experiments.
